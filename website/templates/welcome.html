Early Days' mission is to guide users through an engaging process to indentify personal relationship dealbreakers and preferences.

In an era of increasingly fast-paced dating dynamics, navigating the early stages of getting to know a potential partner has its complexities, particularly when it comes to assessing compatibility with those prospective partners. Early Days aims to provide a solution to this issue so that everyone can feel empowered to determine their compatibility and acknowledge dealbreakers shamelessly.

The curation of the twenty-five questions carefully crafted and selcted for the quiz, focus on the dealbreakers that individuals have within relationships. This is an intentional decision as humans naturally have a negativity bias in their design. This is a psychological phenomenon that has aided the pursuit of crafting the questions, playing on the heightened reaction that humans have to negative stimuli. A common example of this phenomenon at play is how people tend to fixate on insults over praise, or how people often find it easier to point out what they dislike about a person over what they like.

Early Days aims to help individuals to decide for themselves if going forward and pursuing a connection with someone is worthwhile. We do so by assessing the true compatibility between a potential pairing without neglecting the real-life factors that most people take into consideration when evaluating a partner. The overarching goal is to aid individuals in their recognition of potential areas of conflict that may emerge down the line. By prompting these critical topics of discussion at an early stage, the exhaustion of time, energy and emotions can be prevented.
